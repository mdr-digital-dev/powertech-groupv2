PowerTech at ISC West 2025: What We Saw, What's Coming, and What It Means for Your Business

By PowerTech Group of Chicago  ·  April 2025  ·  7 min read

Every spring, the security industry converges on Las Vegas for ISC West — the largest physical security trade show in North America. This year, our team made the trip out to the Venetian Expo to see what's new, meet with our key vendor partners, and get a first look at the technology that will be coming to Chicago businesses in the months ahead.

ISC West is a big deal. Thousands of security professionals, manufacturers, installers, and integrators fill the floor for three days of product launches, live demos, and honest conversations about where the industry is going. After 20+ years in this business, we still look forward to it every year — because the pace of change in security technology is genuinely fast, and staying current is what allows us to deliver the best solutions to our clients.

Here's what stood out from ISC West 2025.

The big theme of 2025: Cloud-native everything. AI-powered analytics moving from enterprise to mid-market. And communications redundancy becoming non-negotiable after too many alarm failures tied to single-path connectivity.

The Vendors That Impressed Us Most

Access Control

Salto Systems — Rethinking How Buildings Handle Access

Salto has been doing impressive things in access control for years, and their ISC West showing reinforced why we're paying close attention to them. Their platform takes a cloud-first, wireless-first approach to access control that's genuinely different from the traditional hardwired systems most Chicago buildings still run. Salto's electronic door hardware works without requiring a full wired infrastructure overhaul — a significant advantage for older commercial buildings, multi-tenant facilities, and retrofits where running cable is costly or disruptive.

What impressed us most was the depth of their ecosystem: from standard office doors to elevators, parking garages, and lockers — all managed from a single cloud platform with real-time event logging, mobile credentials, and integrations with major video surveillance systems. For our clients looking to modernize access control without a full infrastructure project, Salto is going to be a conversation we have more often.

Intrusion · Access · Fire · Cloud Cameras

Alarm.com — One Platform Just Got a Lot More Powerful

Alarm.com is already one of our most-deployed platforms for commercial intrusion and access control, so we always pay close attention to what they're bringing to ISC West. This year, they had a lot to show.

The headline announcement for us: Alarm.com's new Fire Alarm Radio with dual-path communication — LAN and LTE. Starting this year, we'll be deploying this on new fire alarm installations. Dual-path means your fire system maintains its connection to the monitoring center over both your internet connection and cellular simultaneously. If one path goes down — whether from a power outage, a cut line, or a network failure — the other kicks in automatically. For commercial clients with compliance requirements, this is a meaningful upgrade from single-path setups that have historically been a vulnerability.

They also showcased expanded camera capabilities with new doorbell and cloud camera hardware, plus tighter integration between their intrusion, access, and video products. The direction is clear: a single Alarm.com-powered system that covers your whole facility without patchwork from multiple vendors.

Video Surveillance

OpenEye — AI Analytics Priced for the Mid-Market

For years, AI-powered video analytics — object detection, loitering alerts, crowd counting, license plate recognition — were largely the domain of enterprise deployments with enterprise budgets. OpenEye is changing that. Their cloud-managed camera platform delivers genuine AI analytics in a package that makes sense for mid-market Chicago businesses: warehouses, multi-location retailers, healthcare facilities, and commercial property managers.

The practical use cases are compelling. Instead of reviewing hours of footage after an incident, OpenEye's analytics let you search by event type — "show me every time someone entered through the south door after 9pm" — and surface relevant clips in seconds. Combine that with their cloud management interface and you get enterprise-grade situational awareness without needing a dedicated security operations team to run it.

We're actively evaluating OpenEye for clients who need more than passive recording but aren't ready for a full Avigilon or Mobotix enterprise deployment.

Fire Protection

Kidde Commercial — The Evolve Panel Is a Genuine Leap Forward

We already feature Kidde Commercial's Evolve fire alarm system on our site — it's a product we believe in — and seeing it live on the ISC West floor reinforced that confidence. The Evolve panel is Kidde's next-generation addressable fire alarm system, and it genuinely delivers on the "powerfully simple" promise in the marketing.

For our clients, what matters most: Evolve is designed to simplify commissioning and ongoing maintenance without sacrificing the reliability and compliance capabilities that UL Listed commercial fire systems require. The panel's interface is significantly more intuitive than legacy systems, which reduces installation time and — critically — reduces the chance of configuration errors that can cause false alarms or missed events. For buildings moving off aging panels, Evolve is the upgrade path we're recommending.

The Broader Trends We're Watching

AI is moving down-market fast. Two years ago, "AI-powered security" meant a six-figure enterprise contract. This year at ISC West, AI analytics were embedded in products at every price point. The practical implication for Chicago businesses: the gap between basic CCTV and genuinely intelligent surveillance is closing quickly, and the cost to upgrade is lower than most owners expect.

Cloud-native is now the baseline. On-premise servers and proprietary closed systems are increasingly the exception rather than the rule. Nearly every major platform we saw at ISC West leads with cloud management, mobile access, and open API integrations. For business owners, this translates to simpler management, easier scalability, and less dependency on a single vendor's hardware ecosystem.

Communications redundancy is finally being taken seriously. The Alarm.com dual-path fire radio announcement is part of a broader industry movement. Too many alarm failures in recent years have traced back to single-path dependencies — one cable, one cellular carrier, one internet connection. Dual-path and multi-path communication is becoming a standard recommendation, not an upsell.

Integration is everything. The clearest pattern across the entire show floor: the vendors winning are the ones who integrate well with everything else. Open platforms beat closed ones. Buyers — including us — are no longer willing to manage five separate systems with five separate dashboards. The future is unified.

What This Means for Our Chicago Clients

We don't attend ISC West to collect brochures. We go to make informed decisions about what technologies we're going to stake our reputation on and deploy for our clients. Coming out of 2025, the updates you'll see from us:

  • Salto access control as a new option for clients who need wireless, cloud-native door hardware without a full infrastructure overhaul
  • Alarm.com dual-path fire radio standard on new fire alarm installations requiring monitored communication
  • OpenEye cloud cameras as an AI analytics option for mid-market commercial clients
  • Kidde Evolve as our recommended fire alarm panel for new installations and panel replacements

If any of these technologies are relevant to your current setup — or if you're wondering whether what you have today is keeping pace with what's available — we're happy to have that conversation. That's exactly what a free site assessment is for.
